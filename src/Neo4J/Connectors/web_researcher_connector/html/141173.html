Peter Cowman

My research explores patterns and rates of molecular evolution in a phylogenetic comparative framework.  Within this framework, I examine how substitution rates are association with various life history traits and net rates of speciation.  Three area of focus are:

 1. Fecundity and Lifespan: exploring previously reported positive association of fecundity and substitution rates, and the correlation between lifespan and mutation rates.

 2. Lifestyle: exploring if higher rates of molecular evolution in parasites are a consequence of release from constraints faced by free-living relatives, or increased selection on variability due to host-parasite arms race.  Environmental factors and behavioural traits will also be investigated for their influence on species-specific substitution rates.

 3. Diversification: exploring the link between rates of molecular evolution and net speciation rates.

 My previous research explored the diversification and evolutionary biogeography of reef associated fishes (see www.petercowman.com for associated papers).
