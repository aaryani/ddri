Researcher's projects

I work on several applied ecology projects,including:

  1. Surrogate ecology. This is a new project funded by an ARC Laureate Fellowship to Prof David Lindenmayer. The project will involve exploring the conceptual and empirical basis of biodiversity surrogates and indicators, and their application to the management of biodiversity and the environment.
  2. Carrion ecology. I am interested in the role of carrion in ecosystems, and how it supports biodiversity and contributes to nutrient recycling. My research on carrion involves collaboration with landscape ecologists, arthropod taxonomists and soil scientists.
  3. Mulligans Flat - Goorooyarroo Woodland Experiment. This project is an experimental restoration study, and is where I completed my doctoral studies on multi-scale beetle diversity patterns. The project is examining the effects of multiple interacting treatments (log addition, grazing, fire, bettong reintroduction) on multiple groups of organisms (soil microbes, fungi, invertebrates, reptiles, small mammals, birds, plants). I continue to be involved with the invertebrate and soil ecology aspects of this study.
