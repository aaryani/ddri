Is this you? Update your profile

 

My research focuses on the use of molecular tools to improve our understanding of evolutionary forces that shape populations. I am currently studying several avian systems with very different ecologies and life histories with the aim to determine how these differences affect the molecular characterisation of dispersal. I am also interested in the development of novel approaches to analysing dispersal using genetic data. My past research has demonstrated how molecular ecology can be used to quantify and qualify dispersal in invasive populations, with the aim to improve control of this major threat to biodiversity. Additionally, my previous research has illustrated how natural and sexual selection influence genetic outcomes on both individual and population levels.
