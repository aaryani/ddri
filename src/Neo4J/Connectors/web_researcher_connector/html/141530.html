    About me

    I have a background in spatial ecology, and my research specialisations are in population genetics, landscape genetics and GIS based distributional modeling in the context of the applied conservation of wild populations. The central theme of my work to date, has been the effect of colonisation, reintroduction, translocation, migration and scale on genetic and spatial arrangements of wild populations including: freshwater and terrestrial invertebrates, mammals, amphibians and avian species, particularly raptors and seabirds. Increasingly, this has involved looking at these distributions from the perspective of human modified landscapes with particular reference to the effects of climate and ecosystem quality.

    Current projects

    My current projects revolve around a number of avian and mammal projects, some of which are being led by research students (see the link below). They can be loosely categorised under the following headings:

    • The use of remotely sensed data in seabird nest identification.
    • Landscape genetic analysis in a range of species including Tasmanian Wedgetailed eagles and Sulawesi Ungulate species.
    • Conservation genetic and ecotoxicology studies in Sea Eagle species in Australia and Borneo.
    • The development of novel ecotoxicology tools for the study of contamination in seabirds.
    • Aging studies in Seabird species.
    • Satellite tracking in a range of species including Seabirds, European White Stork and Black Cockatoos.
    • Population genetics and mate choice studies in European White Stork and Eurasian Black Vultures.
    I also have a range of projects concentrated on Western Australia’s offshore islands looking  at: secondary colonisation routes to Australia by target raptor species through the Timor Sea; satellite tracking in seabird species as bioindicators of changes to fishery dynamics; the application of remote sensing technologies to nest and colony locations in target seabirds; demographic studies and the development of age-structure curves using novel chemistries; the development of novel approaches to  ecotoxicology studies in coastal and near-shore seabird species; and the detection and impact of marine subsidies in recovering island habitats.
    I have a long list of available student projects. If interested just send through an email.
