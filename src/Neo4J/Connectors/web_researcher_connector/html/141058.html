Postdoctoral Research Fellow in Spatial Genetics

Dr Aaron Adamack

Academic Qualification(s) B.Sc (UBC), MS Oceanography & Coastal Sciences (LSU), PhD Oceanography & Coastal Sciences (LSU)
Phone (02) 6201 2325
Email Address Aaron.Adamack@canberra.edu.au
  • Professional Interests
  • Publications

Using spatial genetics to study the spread of invasive species. Investigating the effects of anthropogenic stressors (e.g. eutrophication, freshwater diversions, and introduction of invasive species) on aquatic ecosystems and populations using individual-based and ecosystem-based models.

Holleley, C., Nichols, R. A., Whitehead, M. R., Adamack, A., Gunn, M. R., & Sherwin, W. B. (2014). Testing single-sample estimators of effective population size in genetically structured populations. Conservation Genetics, 15, 23-35. DOI: 10.1007/s10592-013-0518-3.

Dimond, W. J., Osborne, W. S., Adamack, A., & Evans , M. (2012). Back to the brink - population decline of the endangered grassland earless dragon (Tympanocryptis pinguicolla) following its rediscovery. Herpetological Conservation and Biology, 7, 132-149.

Adamack, A., Stow, C. A., Mason, D. M., Rozas, L. P., & Minello, T. J. (2012). Predicting the effects of freshwater diversions on juvenile brown shrimp production: A Bayesian-based approach. Marine Ecology Progress Series, 44, 155-173.

Adamack, A., Rose, K. A., Breitburg, D. L., Nice , A. J., & Lung , W. S. (2011). Simulating the effect of hypoxia on bay anchovy egg and larval mortality using coupled watershed, water quality, and individual-based predation models. Marine Ecology Progress Series, 445, 141-160.
