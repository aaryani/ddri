Professional Overview

Areas of Research Interest

Dr McMorran has a general interest in understanding the genetic and molecular mechanisms involved in host-pathogen interactions. He has previously investigated lung disease and the associated bacterial infections that occur in the genetic condition, cystic fibrosis. His current focus is on understanding host mechanisms involved in protection from malaria infection, conducted in collaboration with Professor Simon Foote. Specific projects include understanding how platelets protect from malaria parasite infections of the blood stream, identifying novel genes involved in host protection against infection, and development of novel host-directed therapeutic approaches to treat malaria infection.  All these studies utilise murine and cell culture models, and employ various genetic, cell biology and protein chemistry methods.

Dr McMorran also conducts studies investigating genetics underlying kidney disease in Tiwi Islanders in collaboration with researchers at the Menzies Research Institute Tasmania, Hobart and University of Queensland.
