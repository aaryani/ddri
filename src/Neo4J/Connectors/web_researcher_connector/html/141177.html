Research & Current Projects

Did you ever consider the possibility that your uni diet of two-minute noodles could influence the health of your future children? Or perhaps more comforting, the possibility that your hours spent studying might positively affect your future children’s ability to learn? Mounting evidence that the environment, experience and phenotype of parents can influence the characteristics of their offspring – a phenomenon known as “nongenetic inheritance” – has caused a paradigm shift in our understanding of evolution. I am collaborating with Russell Bonduriansky to empirically assess the causes and consequences of nongenetic inheritance using the Australian neriid fly,Telostylinus angusticollis.  In particular, I am interested in investigating the potential of the father’s environment to affect his offspring, and how paternal effects may be mediated by plasticity in ejaculate quality.

 Sperm within the female reproductive tract of the neriid fly Telostylinus angusticollis
