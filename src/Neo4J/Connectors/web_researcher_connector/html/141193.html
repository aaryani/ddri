Professor Mark Dowton

BSc (Hons), Sydney University, PhD, Sydney University

Email:                  biology-enquiries@uow.edu.au
Lab Phone:       +61 2 4221 3438
Office Phone:  +61 2 4221 3013
Lab:                       B35.103
Office:                  B35.G19C  

Position

  • Professor
  • Head of School

Research Interests

  • Mitochondrial genome biology
  • Recombination in mitochondria
  • Evolutionary relationships among Hymenoptera
  • Phylogenetic analysis
  • Mitochondrial gene rearrangements
  • Molecular diagnostics of forensically important insects

Representative Publications

 Mao M, Valerio A, Austin AD, Dowton M, Johnson NF (2012) The first mitochondrial genome for the wasp superfamily Platygastroidea: the egg parasitoid Trissolcus basalis. Genome 55: 194-204.

Hoolahan AH, Blok VC, Gibson T, Dowton M (2012) A comparison of three molecular markers for the identification of populations of Globodera pallida. Journal of Nematology 44: 7-17.

Meiklejohn KA,∙ Dowton M, Wallman JF (2012) Notes on the distribution of 31 species of Sarcophagidae (Diptera) in Australia, including new records in Australia for eight species. Transactions of the Royal Society of South Australia 136:56-64.

Hoolahan AH, Gibson T, Phillips MS, Blok VC, Dowton M (2012) Evidence of animal mtDNA recombination between divergent populations of the potato cyst nematode Globodera pallida. Genetica 140: 20-29.

Meiklejohn KA, Wallman JF,∙Cameron S, Dowton M (2012) Comprehensive evaluation of DNA barcoding for the molecular species identification of forensically important Australian Sarcophagidae (Diptera). Invertebrate Systematics 26: 515-525.

Nelson LA, Lambkin CL, Batterham P, Wallman JF, Dowton M, Whiting MF, Yeates DK, Cameron SL (2012) Beyond barcoding: A mitochondrial genomics approach to molecular phylogenetics and diagnostics of blowflies (Diptera: Calliphoridae). Gene 511: 131-142.

Meiklejohn KA, Wallman JF, Dowton M (2013) DNA barcoding identifies all immature life stages of a forensically important flesh fly (Diptera: Sarcophagidae). Journal of Forensic Science 58: 184-187.

Meiklejohn KA, Dowton M, Pape T, Wallman JF (2013) A key to the Australian Sarcophagidae (Diptera) with special emphasis on Sarcophaga (sensu lato). Zootaxa in press (March 2013).

Meiklejohn KA, Wallman JF,∙Cameron S, Dowton M (2013) Utility of COI, CAD and morphological data for resolving relationships within the genus Sarcophaga (sensu lato) (Diptera: Sarcophagidae): a preliminary study. Molecular Phylogenetics and Evolution in press (1st May, 2013)

Suggested Topics for Future Students

  1. Can complete mitochondrial genome sequences be used to trace the evolutionary relationships among the Hymenoptera, one of the largest insect orders?
  2. How do animal mitochondrial genomes recombine?
  3. The evolution of social groups in coral reef fishes
