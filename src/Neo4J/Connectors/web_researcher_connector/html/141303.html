Brief Summary

Professor Jiwa has 26 years experience as a medical practitioner in primary care in Ireland, the UK and Australia.

His leadership roles have included developing innovations for primary care, leading health policy, representing primary care at the interface between government and specialist services in Australia and overseas.

Professor Jiwa's research focuses on improving the diagnosis and management of chronic conditions in primary care as the foundation of an effective healthcare system. The research uses data collected from clinical practice to look for evidence of unmet need which affects the long term health outcomes for patients.

It focuses on clinical conditions, from health seeking behaviour to symptom assessment, from treatment to follow up. The resulting innovations help health care providers to work together for the benefit of patients.

Overview

Pioneered Telephone Triage in General Practice

Identified 'The Spring Cleaning Effect' —the quantifiable seasonal demand for appointments in General Practice.

Founded the Australasian Medical Journal.

Designed the 'Cancer Care Coordinator'—a model of care to support treatment for people with cancer closer to home.

Demonstrated the impact of written communication between doctors on the care of patients.

Research on the impact of stethoscopes was celebrated worldwide
