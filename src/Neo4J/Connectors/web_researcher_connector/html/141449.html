Profile

Biography

I grew up in South Africa and completed my undergrad and honours degrees there (University of Natal). I then migrated far north to Sweden to undertake a PhD at Göteborg University looking at sexual selection in the amazing African widowbirds. After completing my PhD in 2003 I had a bit of time off from academia before moving to Australia to take up a New South Global Postdoctoral Fellowship at the University of New South Wales (Sydney) in 2005. Here I started working on the intriguing and gaudy Gouldian finch. Still continuing with the Gouldian I moved to Macquarie University (Sydney) in 2007 with an ARC Postdoctoral Fellowship (APD) and in 2010 took up a Macquarie Vice-Chancellor Innovation Research Fellowship. Finally, after nearly 7 years of postdocing I was lucky enough to recently land a real job here at ANU.

Research interests

I'm broadly interested in the evolutionary and ecological consequences of behaviour, including the evolution of alternative reproductive strategies, colour polymorphism, mate choice, male competition, multiple mating, parental care, maternal effects, physiological adaptations, speciation and conservation.

I mainly work with tropical Australian finches, including crimson finches, long-tailed finches, parrot finches and especially the unique little Gouldian finch. I have also recently expanded my study systems to include tropical Australian reptiles, especially the colourful and charismatic frill-necked lizard. 

My research uses a combination of captive and field studies. Most of the fieldwork is undertaken in the eastern Kimberley of Western Australia, a very special and remote area of Australia.
