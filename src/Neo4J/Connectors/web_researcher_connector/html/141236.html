Dr Todd Gaines

Powerpoint presentation

Rapporteur: Biochemical and molecular basis ofresistance

Biography

Dr Todd Gaines is a Postdoctoral Research Associate at theUniversity of Western Australia (UWA) contracted to BayerCropScience (Germany) for 2012. Dr Gaines is currently researchingthe molecular basis of herbicide resistance, including theidentification of genes conferring non-target site based resistancein annual ryegrass (Lolium rigidum).

Dr Gaines has a BSc, MSc and PhD from Colorado State University.Dr Gaines’ PhD research on the emerging issue of the evolution ofglyphosate resistance in plants has been widely published inreputable journals. In this study Dr Gaines made important researchbreakthroughs as he discovered a new and unexpected molecularmechanism that endows resistance to glyphosate in plants. DrGaines’ discovery showed that resistant plants displayed up to 100fold amplification of the EPSPS gene. This gene amplification actedas a “molecular sponge” endowing high level resistance toglyphosate. This was a ground-breaking and elegant piece ofresearch that he published as first author in the prestigiousjournal PNAS.  

Dr Gaines has authored or co-authored nine journal papers and 45conference papers. His major research achievement includespublication in PNAS (Gaines et al. 2010) and EvolutionaryApplications (Gaines et al. 2012) a new and highly rankedevolutionary biology journal. Added to this are publications inWeed Science and Crop Science.

On completion of his postgraduate qualifications in Colorado,USA, Dr Gaines spent three years as a post-doc with the AustralianHerbicide Resistance Initiative (AHRI) at UWA in Perth, Australia,and worked on a new herbicide molecule, pyroxasulfone. He conductedexperiments to evaluate the potential for plants to evolveresistance to this herbicide.
